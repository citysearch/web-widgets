Pomp C.

San Luis Obispo, CA

8/17/2010

Covell is a great concept and has potential to be one of the coolest wine stops in LA.

The decor seems to have been mentioned quite a bit in other reviews, but I'll add my voice: the place looks cool inside. It's kind of like a bistro meets a barn meets an antique shop meets your friend's living room, if your friend had money for a sweet beer tap.

The wine selection was very interesting, stocked more with lesser-known regions and styles than most wine bars. I guess that would make it sort of an adventurous list. My friends and I enjoyed most of the 6 or 7 wines we sampled and though the format is typically by-the-glass, we were kindly accommodated with a flight. The beer selection is also very solid.

The food is taken seriously and we enjoyed a cheese plate that amongst other tasty items, included a local creamery that made killer cheeses we'd never tried before. It fed three people and at $18.00 seemed reasonable for its quality.

One interesting aspect of the concept is that there seems to be a heavy emphasis on the staff. By this I mean, it seems the employees are meant to be the menu, meant to engage the customers and ultimately, are sort of meant to steer the customers' decisions. There isn't really an easily accessible menu and perhaps some patrons may be put off by relying on their server so much.

On the whole, I really enjoyed Covell, though, and I'd recommend it for any one who enjoys good wine, beer and a social ambiance.

Frances L.

Los Angeles, CA

8/6/2010 2 photos

Holy crapperfiddles, Batman! I've found a new cave to hide in! Covell opened up just a month or so ago and it's been welcomed into Los Feliz with wide, open arms out-stretched to the very ends of the universe. I had heard good things, but let me tell you, this place is worthy of GREAT things. First of all, the space itself is cozy and intimate, but not super congested (think bigger than Tiki Ti and the Frolic Room, but not by much). The ambiance was dark and the room was aglow with votive candles and the reflections of the beautiful (and tasty) wall of wine bottles at the bar. Prices are definitely fair for beer and wine; mostly delicious microbrews and wine that was hand-picked by the owners themselves.

We walked into the semi-crowded bar and immediately found some counter-space. Dustin, one of the owners asked us what we wanted and I immediately took advantage of his know-how to open up a very helpful dialog about what I was in the mood for. Within seconds, my key words had gotten me a wonderful glass of some 2007 Pinot Noir from Oregon. He showed me the bottle before he opened it and gave me a taste to see if I liked it, but it was so dark, I can't remember which vineyard it was from. Boyfriend got the Port Brewing Double IPA which smelled fantastic.

The people seemed like an eclectic group of Hollywood/Los Feliz/ Silver Lake locals and mostly kept to their own corners. Saw a couple B-listers hanging out and talking shop in the corner and made friends with a nice man who apparently, only lived up the street. Boyfriend spotted Paul Thomas Anderson outside and I was proud of him for not geeking out right then and there.

Covell started doing small plates a couple of weeks ago, so I'll be sure to update the Yelp world on that as the grilled cheese that came out smelled amazing. My glass of wine was exactly what I needed to unwind-- something with a medium body that was more fruity than it was spicy, with just the right amount of earthiness to it.

I'm in love.

7/25/2010 1 photo 2 Check-ins Here

So, I'm not ashamed that I arrived here 6 minutes before they opened on a Sunday early evening (5pm) and didn't retire until 9pm.

Now, don't get the wrong idea; I'm no lush or drunkard, I was on a laptop the entire time so I was more occupied by a lit screen than lit patrons.

When I walked in I asked if they had any outlets I could use and unfortunately they had one that was really far behind the bar and not so convenient for enjoying a bevi while working. However, upon sitting down a discovery was made! There just so happened to be an outlet in close proximity to where I declared my workspace to be, so I plugged in and started typing away. I left the choice of my first glass to the handsome gentleman behind the bar and just told him that I am a beer girl, but if he preferred to serve me wine (given that it is a wine bar) that it should be a white varietal. He let me sample his selection and I gave his Vinho Verde (per the receipt) the "that'll do." I took more than two hours to sip it down, as I was too engrossed in my tedious tasks to give it the full attention it deserved.

As I laptoped away, the bar got really, really crowded. It was insane for a Sunday afternoon, but all the more testimony that Covell is a charming venue on a strip of Hollywood Boulevard I wouldn't frequent otherwise. I eventually ordered a beer (I was very tempted to order a few of their menu items but figured a liquid meal was best suited for a Sunday evening). The triple IPA selection was tasty indeed. I felt light-headed after it (probably the lack of savory, but I digress). I loved it. L-O-V-E, loved it. I even ordered another one, even though I shouldn't have. I looked like quite the anomaly; trudging away at my responsibilities at hand, but the gents behind the bar took care of me like any other needy patron.

The decor is vintage camera-chic. Clearly you'll have to visit yourself for a finer description than mine. I am a little disappointed at the fact that I was there for countless hours and didn't feel like a regular, but then again, I was the one plugging away like I had no interest in being entertained, so I can't really hold that against these fine chaps. If this place was walking distance I would be here more often than not.

Love this place!  I want to go back right now!

This spot couldn't be any cuter and couldn't be more approachable for wine lovers, wine beginners or just those that want a new spot to gather with friends. They really did everything right here.  From the vintage decor to chalk board wine list, you feel at home and at ease and really have to force yourself to leave.

We dropped in on a Saturday afternoon around 5 and enjoyed a couple glasses of wine and a charcuterie plate.  The guys behind the bar are super helpful and will guide you into trying something new based on your preferences.  The selection is diverse from South American Tannat to French Carignan with the more widely known Malbecs, Zins and everything else in between.  

The food menu is short- cheese plate, charcuterie or a croque-type sandwich.  We had the charcuterie which was 5-6 different meats, pickled spicy peppers, mustard, cornichons and 3 slices of toasted baguette.  This was enough to hold us over until our late dinner.

The crowd was definitely local, and definitely wine people.  There was a point with standing room only, so overall, the place has gotten a lot of good initial interest.

I will most definitely come back as the space couldn't be more comfortable and conducive to enjoying your friend's company.

I love this kind of place - serious wine/beer-only bar, but not so stuffy that you feel like you have to whisper between sips of an overpriced bottle. Also, since I had lost my wallet a few nights before, I came extra prepared with a temporary driver license (scrap of paper from the DMV), my checkbook (only other thing I owned with my name on it), and a copy of my graduation photo (if I finished college I have to be at least 21, right?!). Thankfully, the bartender didn't get that far. After I told him my situation, he quickly covered up my birthdate on the DMV paper and blurted "When were you born?!" April 5th, 1987!! "OK, you're good." Thank God... Covell still has some of that just-opened energy, and none of the quiet emptiness indicative of a soon-to-fail restaurant. It was noisily crowded with people, but not in an uncomfortable way. And the little patio out front is the best. I have to admit, I do get a little uneasy around the slick hipsters who populate cross-town rival El Prado wine bar in Echo Park. Covell just seems friendlier, though I guess both places have their unique advantages.

Adorable wine bar on an up-and-coming stretch of Hollywood.

These guys know what's up with good wine (and beer).  And they let you experience and taste it in a casual and cool setting.  Nice bar, glassware, service and music.

All my best, Covell -- I hope you're here to stay and pour for a while.
